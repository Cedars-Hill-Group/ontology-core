To encode the logic our of business we consider three distinct entities at all times: People, Companies/Organizations, and Properties.

Companies or Organizations are groupings of people and resources. These can be public or private, governmental agencies, countries, etc. 

Properties are a grouping of natural resources in a distinct geographic location. These can be residential or commercial real estate, raw land, mines, ports, airports, municipal service facilities, etc.

People are a system primitive. Companies and properties are made up of other primitives.

We utilize an ontology to define the system of relations between these different entities in the real world. The ontology defines the objects within the system, their actions, links, and properties. An object is distinct from an entity in that an entity is a unique primitive or grouping of other primitives whereas objects can be anything in the system. When we identify an object we first want to determine if it is an entity. After that determination has been made we then want to understand how the object interacts within the system which is determined by its properties, actions, and links. This determines its relationship to other objects and entities within the system.

The ontology is adaptable to new information and relationships being constrained only by the laws of physics. For every object or entity in the system we look at four core economic activities that it performs:
1. Procurement (ie. Origination, Sourcing, etc)
2. Make (ie. Production, Manufacturing, etc)
3. Managing
4. Selling and Exchanging
